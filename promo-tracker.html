Lead Designer Expanded Scope Systems Design

Bringing clarity to how savings work at Shipt.

Promos and coupons powered savings across Shipt’s marketplace — but they looked identical, behaved differently, and gave members no way to track what they could save. I audited the system, unified the framework, and designed a savings tracker that surfaces opportunities as members shop.

The Audit

I was asked to make eligible items easier to find. I found a bigger problem.

The original ask was narrow — reduce the clicks to view eligible items for promos and coupons. But when I mapped the full experience, I found systemic inconsistency. Promos and coupons looked identical on product cards but behaved completely differently. Clicking a promo took you to the PDP. Clicking a coupon showed eligible items. Progress tracking worked differently for each. The visual language was the same, but the interaction model diverged at every touchpoint.

In product cards — promos weren’t clickable, coupons were. But they looked exactly the same.

On PDPs — promos, pre-clipped coupons, and post-clipped coupons all looked and behaved differently.

What I Found
#1

Promos & coupons looked identical, but behaved very differently.

#2

Members had no way to actively track or pursue savings. The experience was entirely passive.

Both problems traced back to the same root: the system had no unified framework for how savings mechanisms should look, behave, and communicate across surfaces.

Expanding the Brief

I reframed the project from a single-feature fix to a three-part systems redesign:

1.

Simplify access

Reduce a 5-step flow to 2. Members should go from product card to eligible items in two taps.

2.

Unify the experience

One interaction model for promos and coupons across all surfaces. Members shouldn’t have to learn two behaviors.

3.

Surface savings proactively

Build a tracker that shows members what they can save as they shop — not after.

One System, Not Two

I aligned how promos and coupons behave at every level. Promos now show a chevron for visual affordance of clickability. Both types lead to a consistent details page with eligible items shown immediately. PDP states — default, in progress, complete — follow the same pattern regardless of savings type.

From Five Steps to Two

The original coupon flow required tapping the coupon, clipping it, waiting for transitions, tapping mix and match, then viewing eligible items. The redesigned flow: tap the promo or coupon from the product card, see eligible items immediately.

Savings Tracker

Savings shouldn’t be something members stumble into.

I designed a consistent potential savings tracker that appears across the shopping journey — on the retailer home page, in search results, and in cart. It surfaces applicable promos and coupons based on items already added, shows progress toward thresholds, and prompts actionable next steps. The tracker transforms savings from a passive discovery into an active, guided experience.
